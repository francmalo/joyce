Eulogy

A Life of Grace & Purpose

She made the world feel safer, kinder, and more worth living in — simply by being in it.

Joyce Katuki came into this world on a bright March morning, the youngest of four children born to Thomas and Clara in the rolling hills of Vermont. From her earliest days she carried a warmth that made every room feel smaller and more intimate — a gift she never once withheld.

She trained as a schoolteacher and spent thirty-two faithful years shaping young minds at Millbrook Elementary, where generations of children learned not only to read and multiply, but to be kind, to be curious, and to be brave. Parents who once sat in her classroom brought their own children to her, and she greeted each small face with the same unhurried smile that had never aged a single day.

Beyond the classroom she was a devoted wife to Harold for fifty-one years, a mother who always listened first and spoke second, and a grandmother whose cookie jar was never empty and whose back door was forever unlocked. Her garden was legendary — a riot of dahlias and sweet peas she tended before dawn, while the rest of the world still slept.

In her final years, even as her body grew quieter, her spirit refused any such diminishment. She knitted blankets for the hospital, wrote long letters by hand, and laughed — genuinely and completely — at the smallest of pleasures. She taught us, through the simple testimony of her days, that an extraordinary life need not be a loud one.

We do not mourn her passing so much as we celebrate the extraordinary privilege of having known her. Joyce did not leave this world smaller; she left it richer, tenderer, better. And we carry her forward in every act of gentleness we choose to make our own.

Life Story

Milestones & Memories

  • 1942

    Born in Burlington, Vermont

    The fourth child of Thomas and Clara, Joyce arrived on a snow-dusted March morning. Her father said she was smiling before the doctor had finished counting her fingers.

  • 1964

    Graduated from University of Vermont

    Earned her Bachelor of Education with highest honours. Her thesis on childhood literacy was cited by the state board as a model of compassionate pedagogy.

  • 1967

    Married Harold James

    A June wedding beneath the oak trees her father had planted. Harold called the day perfect; Joyce said the cake was slightly too sweet — and she was probably right.

  • 1970–2002

    Thirty-Two Years at Millbrook Elementary

    She taught four hundred and twelve children by her own careful count. Three became doctors, one a novelist — all remembered her name for the rest of their lives.

  • 1994

    Vermont Teacher of the Year

    Recognised statewide for exceptional contribution to early-childhood education. She used every penny of the award to buy books for the school library.

  • 2017

    Golden Wedding Anniversary

    A quiet celebration surrounded by children, grandchildren, and the oak trees under which she and Harold had made their vows fifty years earlier.

  • 2024

    Returned to the Light

    Peacefully, at home, held by family. Her final words were a quiet thank you — entirely characteristic of a woman who spent her whole life grateful.
