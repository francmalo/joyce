Eulogy

A Life of Faith & Love

"Your love remains a guiding light and a source of strength in our hearts. You've left a legacy of hard work, love, kindness and compassion."

Joyce Katuki Allan was born in the year 1962, the fourth child of Mama Phyllis Kaswii and Mutinda Nzau, in Mwania Village, Mulango location. She grew up surrounded by her siblings — MaryRose, Ngita, John, Serah, Kithúkú, and the late Nzioka — and was a sister-in-law to many, and an aunt to countless more.

Early Life & Education

She completed her primary education at Wií Primary School, and her ambition and drive led her to Mombasa Tailoring College, where she honed a craft that would define much of her working life. Between 1985 and 1986, she shared her knowledge generously, teaching at Ngumau Tailoring School. Joyce eventually settled in Kitui town, where she ran her own business while simultaneously mentoring and training young tailors.

Marriage & Family

In the late 1980s, Joyce Katuki married her beloved husband, Allan Ndumbu. Together they built a home full of love, laughter, and purpose. She is survived by her five children — Bonke, Miranda, Best, Kelvin, and Sheldon — as well as her son-in-law Moses Nzioka and daughters-in-law Dinah, Dorothy, Editah, and Peninah. Joyce was also the proud and doting grandmother of thirteen grandchildren: Arianna, Stancy, Joy, Vicky, Nala, Niara, Adaya, Junior, Wema, Hawi, Zuberi, Amali, and Jeyden.

Faith & Christian Life

Joyce was born and raised in a Christian family, attending Sunday school from her earliest years and eventually being baptised at AIC Church Mwania, where she served as a choir member. She was a lead alto during the Malombe family worship gatherings. In her later years, she became a strong and steadfast member of Rhema Fellowship Church Wikililye, remaining faithful in her walk until her very last days.

A Courageous Journey

In late 2024, Joyce began to feel unwell, initially seeking care at Kitui General Hospital before moving to Nairobi for specialised treatment. The doctors there diagnosed her with heart failure, and she underwent care at Metropolitan Hospital, MP Shah, and Westlands Specialists Hospital. Through it all, Joyce fought with extraordinary bravery and an unwavering faith in God. On Saturday, 7th March 2026, the Almighty God called her home at Luton Hospital in Nairobi.

We thank God for her life, and for the wholehearted service she rendered here on earth. She poured herself into her children, her grandchildren, her church, and every young person she taught. May her beautiful soul rest in eternal peace.
